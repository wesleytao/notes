## Nutrition Plan

### The Cheat Meal Problem

Your weekly family cheat meal at ~1,500 kcal is currently unmanaged. At a 500 kcal/day deficit, one 1,500 kcal meal wipes out roughly 2â€“3 days of progress depending on how it lands.

**The fix is not "eliminate family dinners."** The fix is:

- **Phase 1:** Reframe it as a structured refeed. Eat higher carbs, keep protein high, keep fat moderate. Enjoy the meal but track it approximately. Budget for it by keeping the rest of that day slightly lower.
- **Phase 2:** Tighten it. The meal still happens but within a calorie ceiling (~800â€“1,000 kcal over your rest day target, mostly carbs).
- **Phase 3:** It becomes your planned refeed and nothing more. Or you shift it to a meal that fits your macros. This is the sacrifice phase.

**Non-negotiable:** At no point does this meal go untracked. "I'll just eat clean the rest of the week" is how you showed up 4 weeks behind last time.

- Protein stays high throughout. Never cut protein to create the deficit.
- Fats have a floor (~40g minimum). Hormones, joints, and brain function need them.
